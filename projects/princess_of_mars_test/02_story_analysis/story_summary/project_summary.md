# Project Summary: John Carter on Mars (Thark City Arc)

## Setting and Context
The story takes place in Thark, a major community among the green Martians. The protagonist, John Carter, holds a unique dual status: he is a prisoner of the Tharks but has been granted chieftain rank due to his combat prowess. He wears the metal (weapons) of slain chieftains, signifying his authority within Tharkian society.

## Key Characters and Relationships
*   **John Carter:** An alien from Earth who commands respect but is not fully accepted as one of the Tharks. He is currently under surveillance by Lorquas Ptomel.
*   **Dejah Thoris:** The "red girl," granddaughter of a red jeddak (enemy of the Tharks). She has been captured and transferred to Tal Hajus, the Grand Jeddak of the Red Martians. Her safety is the primary concern for John Carter.
*   **Lorquas Ptomel:** A high-ranking Thark warrior who oversees John Carter's status. He warns Carter of accusations regarding escape plots involving Dejah Thoris.
*   **Tars Tarkas:** Lorquas Ptomel's trusted lieutenant and a powerful figure behind the throne. He maintains a neutral demeanor toward Carter but respects his fighting ability.
*   **Tal Hajus:** The Grand Jeddak of the Red Martians, described as cruel and calculating. He is the ultimate authority over Dejah Thoris's fate.

## Current Plot Stakes
John Carter faces immediate persecution due to rumors that he plotted to escape with Dejah Thoris. These rumors could lead to his execution if proven. However, he is granted a trial on their return to Thark. The safe delivery of Dejah Thoris to Tal Hajus is critical for the Tharks' honor. John Carter realizes that some horrible fate awaits her at Tal Hajus's headquarters and feels an absolute necessity for escape.

## Social Structure
Thark society operates under strict communal rules regarding property, mating, and retinues. All property is owned in common except personal weapons, ornaments, and sleeping silks/furs. Women in a man's retinue are not wives but military units under his responsibility for instruction, discipline, and sustenance. This structure dictates how John Carter interacts with the women assigned to him.

## Visual Continuity Notes
*   **Audience Chamber:** Lorquas Ptomel signals advance; great hideous eyes fix on Carter.
*   **Plaza/Corridor:** Tars Tarkas approaches with unchanged demeanor; guides Carter to a building adjoined by Sola's quarters.
*   **Quarters Selection:** Carter ascends winding corridor; chooses front room on third floor for proximity to Dejah Thoris (second floor).
*   **Arrival of Women:** Young females bearing loads of weapons, silks, furs, and food enter; place goods in back rooms; depart and return with second load.
