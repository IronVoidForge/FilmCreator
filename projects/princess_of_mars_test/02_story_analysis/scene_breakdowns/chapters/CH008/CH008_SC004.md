# SC004 - Earth Girl Reveal & Emotional Connection

## Scene Purpose
Establish the emotional core through visual contact between Narrator and captive, creating narrative hook.

## Scene Summary
One ship is disabled and captured by the Green Martians. The warriors loot the vessel of arms, food, and valuables, then set it ablaze before towing it away. During the aftermath, the Narrator sees a prisoner being dragged into a building by Green Martian females. The prisoner is revealed to be a slender, girlish figure resembling an earthly woman with coal-black hair and reddish-copper skin. She makes eye contact with the Narrator, signaling hope which fades into dejection when he fails to respond.

## Participating Characters
- Narrator (observer from window)
- Woola (implied presence following close at heel)
- Earth Girl prisoner (slender figure with coal-black hair, reddish-copper skin)
- Green Martian females (dragging prisoner into building)

## Participating Environments
- Building interior (where prisoner is dragged)
- Window view for Narrator's observation
- Plaza entrance area
- Captured vessel vicinity

## Dominant Emotional Shift
Hope → Dejection, unspoken connection, narrative hook established

## Likely Visual Coverage Families
- Close-ups of Earth Girl's face and features
- Over-the-shoulder shots from Narrator's perspective
- Eye contact sequences between characters
- Atmospheric shots emphasizing emotional weight

## Likely Continuity Sensitivities
- Earth Girl's physical description (coal-black hair, reddish-copper skin) must be consistent
- Eye contact timing and duration needs precise execution
- Hope-to-dejection transition must be clear visually
- Narrator's emotional response (or lack thereof) needs subtle indication
