# Scene SC001 - Setup: Extinction Declared

## Purpose
Establish the dire situation facing Barsoom and create emotional stakes for the audience. Show the atmosphere plant failure and Jeddak's declaration of three days remaining before death by asphyxiation becomes inevitable.

## Summary
The atmosphere plant engine has stopped, causing air pressure to decrease rapidly throughout Barsoom. Tardos Mors paces on the raised platform in the Council Chamber while members fill the chamber; silence follows his announcement of doom. The Royal family including Woola gathers in a sunken garden within an inner courtyard as breathing becomes difficult. The Golden Incubator on the roof contains a snow-white egg representing the unborn son, adding emotional weight to the extinction threat.

## Participating Characters
- Tardos Mors (Council leader)
- Council members
- Royal family (including Woola)

## Participating Environments
- Golden Incubator (roof of Helium Palace)
- Council Chamber (raised platform)
- Palace Garden (sunken courtyard within inner courtyard)

## Dominant Emotional Shift
Hope → Despair

The scene begins with a sense of urgency as the atmosphere plant fails, then shifts to despair as the Jeddak declares three days remaining before death becomes inevitable. The royal family's breathing difficulty and the unborn son in the incubator amplify the emotional stakes.

## Likely Visual Coverage Families
- Wide shots establishing Golden Incubator on palace roof
- Medium shots of council chamber with Tardos Mors pacing
- Close-ups of royal family members breathing difficult
- Static shots emphasizing silence after announcement

## Likely Continuity Sensitivities
- Egg continuity in Golden Incubator (snow-white, unborn son representation)
- Silence following Jeddak's announcement (no background noise)
- Breathing difficulty visual cues (chest movement, facial expressions)
- Council chamber raised platform positioning
