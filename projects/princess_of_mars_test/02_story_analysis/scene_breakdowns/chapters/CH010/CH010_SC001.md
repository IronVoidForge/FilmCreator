# SC001 - Exploration Outside City Limits

## Scene Purpose
Establish John Carter's relationship with Woola and introduce the Mars cultural misunderstanding that will have significant consequences.

## Scene Summary
Early in the morning, John Carter explores outside the city limits with Woola, testing the hound's loyalty and affection. He experiences a moment of misunderstanding when he laughs at Woola's playful affection; on Mars, laughter signifies torture and death, causing him to realize his mistake immediately.

## Participating Characters
- **John Carter** - Protagonist exploring Martian territory
- **Woola** - Loyal hound companion showing affection

## Participating Environments
- Outside city limits
- Martian landscape (early morning lighting)

## Dominant Emotional Shift
Affection → Realization of mistake → Cultural awareness

## Likely Visual Coverage Families
- Wide shots establishing the Martian exterior
- Medium shots capturing Carter and Woola's interaction
- Close-ups on facial expressions during the laughter moment
- Reaction shots showing immediate realization

## Likely Continuity Sensitivities
- Lighting consistency (early morning)
- Character positioning relative to city limits
- Woola's behavior patterns throughout scene
