# Scene 003: Valor and Regalia

## Scene Purpose
Show the aftermath of John Carter's intervention, his elevation to chieftain status through regalia, and Tars Tarkas's explanation of customs before departure.

## Scene Summary
Following Martian custom, John Carter is given the dead warrior's regalia and arms, elevating his status as a chieftain. Tars Tarkas explains the customs of combat and captivity to John Carter, noting that his valor has earned him respect but also responsibility for his safe delivery to Tal Hajus. John Carter asserts his humanity and conscience before departing with Dejah Thoris and Woola from the chamber.

## Participating Characters
- John Carter (now wearing regalia)
- Dejah Thoris
- Tars Tarkas
- Other Thark warriors (bringing regalia)

## Participating Environments
- Audience chamber (post-confrontation)
- Steps of rostrum where regalia is presented
- Chamber entrance for departure

## Dominant Emotional Shift
From violent confrontation to respectful elevation; John Carter's status shifts from outsider to chieftain; Tars Tarkas moves from quizzical observation to direct address regarding language skills and valor.

## Likely Visual Coverage Families
- Medium shots of regalia presentation (respectful yet menacing)
- Close-ups of John Carter wearing Thark regalia
- Over-the-shoulder shots during Tars Tarkas's explanation
- Wide shots showing chamber layout for departure sequence
- Detail shots of silk from cape used to staunch flow

## Likely Continuity Sensitivities
- Regalia visibility and placement on John Carter
- Dejah Thoris's injury state (nosebleed) must remain consistent
- Silk from cape continuity when used to staunch flow
- Tars Tarkas's quizzical eye contact during address
