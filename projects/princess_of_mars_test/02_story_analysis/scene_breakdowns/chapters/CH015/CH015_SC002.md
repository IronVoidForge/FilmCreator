# SC002

## Scene Purpose
Explore the emotional conflict between John Carter and Dejah Thoris, revealing her prideful grief and establishing a temporary truce based on mutual respect.

## Scene Summary
Carter visits Sola and Dejah after his recovery. He discovers Dejah is weeping in deep grief, believing him dead due to her prideful nature. She has not spoken about her feelings despite her pain. Carter promises to respect her feelings until she speaks, creating a temporary understanding between them.

## Participating Characters
- John Carter (primary focus)
- Dejah Thoris (emotional core of scene)
- Sola (supporting presence)

## Participating Environments
- Interior meeting space where Carter visits Dejah and Sola
- Location showing Dejah's emotional state through visual cues

## Dominant Emotional Shift
From physical recovery to emotional confrontation. The scene shifts from vulnerability to pride, then to a tentative promise of respect that bridges their conflict.

## Likely Visual Coverage Families
- Close-up shots of Dejah's weeping and emotional pain
- Medium shots showing Carter's concerned expressions
- Two-shot compositions emphasizing the distance between them despite physical proximity

## Likely Continuity Sensitivities
- Dejah's emotional state must remain consistent throughout scene
- Any dialogue about pride and grief needs clear visual reinforcement
- Character positioning should reflect their emotional distance
