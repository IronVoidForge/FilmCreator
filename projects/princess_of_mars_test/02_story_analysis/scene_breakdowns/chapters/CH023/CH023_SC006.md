# Scene 006: Navigation Loss

## Scene Purpose
Establish the new crisis condition after crash. Create tension through blind flight and demonstrate the consequences of instrument destruction.

## Scene Summary
After recovering near the ground, Carter's compass and speedometer are destroyed by the projectile fragment. He flies blindly for nearly six hours. The scene ends with Carter continuing toward destination without navigational instruments.

## Participating Characters
- Carter (protagonist, pilot)

## Participating Environments
- Near Ground Recovery Zone
- Blind flight corridor over terrain
- Sky approaching morning recovery point

## Dominant Emotional Shift
From crisis management to resigned determination. The scene moves from immediate danger to sustained challenge and endurance.

## Likely Visual Coverage Families
- Interior shots showing damaged instruments
- Wide shots establishing blind flight over terrain
- Close-ups on Carter's focused expression during navigation loss
- Time-lapse or montage sequences showing passage of hours
- Sky shots showing lack of visual reference points

## Likely Continuity Sensitivities
- Instrument damage must match across all shots
- Flight path and direction remain consistent throughout sequence
- Any visual markers on machine exterior stay stable
- Passage of time shown consistently in any time-lapse sequences
