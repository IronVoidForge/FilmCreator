# Scene SC001: The Cave Paralysis & Indian Encounter

## Scene Purpose
Establish the narrator's supernatural paralysis and introduce the stealthy threat of approaching Indians. Create cliff-edge tension through visual contrast between the paralyzed narrator and the warriors on the ledge.

## Scene Summary
The chapter opens with the narrator mentally intact but physically paralyzed in a cave, unable to move despite mental faculties working. Approaching horses stop near the opening, revealing stealthy Indians creeping along the cliff ledge. The warriors view the narrator with awe and fear but do not approach. A sound from behind the narrator causes the Indians to flee in terror, one falling off the cliff.

## Participating Characters
- **Narrator:** Paralyzed/turned to stone, mentally aware but physically immobile
- **Indians:** War-bonneted, paint-streaked faces, savage eyes, stealthy approach

## Participating Environments
- **Interior of cave:** Dim light, opening to daylight, vapor filling cave (tenuous)
- **Cliff ledge:** Trail between cave and cliff turn, dangerous drop-off

## Dominant Emotional Shift
**Fear/Awe -> Terror**  
The Indians move from cautious observation to panicked flight when the sound occurs. The narrator remains paralyzed throughout, creating dramatic irony.

## Likely Visual Coverage Families
- **Wide shots:** Cave interior showing paralysis state, horses at opening
- **Medium shots:** Indians on ledge, their war-bonneted appearance and paint-streaked faces
- **Close-ups:** Narrator's paralyzed expression, eyes moving despite body stillness
- **High-angle shots:** Cliff ledge perspective showing dangerous drop-off

## Likely Continuity Sensitivities
- **Narrator's paralysis state:** Must remain immobile throughout scene (no movement of limbs)
- **Indians' approach pattern:** Stealthy creeping motion, not running until climax
- **Horse positioning:** Standing with dragging rein initially at opening
- **Lighting consistency:** Dim cave interior vs daylight at opening
