# Scene 002 - Discovery of Glass Incubator with Hatching Eggs

## Scene Purpose
Introduce the Martians and their reproductive cycle through the narrator's discovery of hatching eggs.

## Scene Summary
The narrator discovers a glass incubator containing giant eggs inside the Mars basin. They observe grotesque young Martians hatching from snowy white eggs (2.5 feet diameter). The hatching process is observed in detail, establishing the alien life forms before adult Martians appear.

## Participating Characters
- Narrator (first-person perspective)
- Young Martians (hatching from eggs)

## Participating Environments
- Glass incubation chamber within Mars basin
- Close proximity to yellowish mosslike vegetation
- Quartz-bearing rock formations visible through glass
- Low hills in background view

## Dominant Emotional Shift
Curiosity and discovery → Awe at alien life forms

## Likely Visual Coverage Families
- Close-up shots of eggs (snowy white, 2.5 feet diameter)
- Medium shots capturing hatching process
- Overhead or angled shots showing incubator interior
- Reaction shots of narrator's facial expressions during observation

## Likely Continuity Sensitivities
- Egg appearance must remain consistent (snowy white, 2.5 feet diameter)
- Hatching process must be shown consistently across shots
- Young Martian physical traits must match established specifications:
  - 15 feet tall
  - Dark slate skin
  - Yellow feet
  - Small cup-shaped antennae ears
  - Blood red iris
  - White teeth with upward-curving tusks
- Glass incubator transparency and appearance must remain consistent
