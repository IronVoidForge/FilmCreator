# SC002

**Scene Purpose:** To reveal the temporal logic of the tea party and the conflict between the Hatter and Time.

**Scene Summary:** The Hatter reveals his watch, which tracks the day but not the hour. He explains that because he quarreled with "Time," it is perpetually tea-time. This explanation establishes the surrealist rules governing their constant movement around the table.

**Participating Characters:**
- Alice
- The Hatter
- The March Hare
- The Dormouse

**Participating Environments:** Under the tree at the tea table.

**Dominant Emotional Shift:** From intellectual debate to a sense of temporal absurdity.

**Likely Visual Coverage Families:**
- Extreme close-up of the Hatter's watch.
- Medium shots of the group reacting to the concept of Time.

**Likely Continuity Sensenstitivities:** The physical handling of the watch; the lighting/time-of-day consistency.
