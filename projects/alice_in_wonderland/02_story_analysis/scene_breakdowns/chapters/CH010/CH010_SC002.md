# SC002

**Scene Purpose:** To contrast Alice's grounded reality with the creatures' nonsensical logic through storytelling and poetry.

**Scene Summary:** The Gryphon prompts Alice to tell her story. As she recounts her adventures, the conversation shifts to a lesson on poetry where Alice accidentally recites a nonsensical version of "'Tis the voice of the sluggard." This sparks a debate between the Mock Turtle and the Gryphon regarding the meaning of the verses.

**Participating Characters:**
* Alice
* The Gryphon
* The Mock Turtle

**Participating Environments:** The same seaside-adjacent environment.

**Dominant Emotional Shift:** From inquisitive storytelling to intellectualized confusion/absurdity.

**Likely Visual Coverage Families:**
* Medium shots of the group listening to Alice.
* Tight close-ups on the Gryphon's authoritative expressions.
* Reaction shots of the Mock Turtle as he processes the "meaning" of the poem.

**Likely Continuity Sensences:** The transition from the physical energy of the dance to a more stationary, conversational grouping.
