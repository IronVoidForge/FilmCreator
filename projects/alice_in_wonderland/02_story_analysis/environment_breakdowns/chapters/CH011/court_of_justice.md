# Court of Justice

**Role:** Primary setting.

**Architecture or Geography:** 
A large, open-air outdoor courtroom or a vast, high-ceilinged hall designed for public spectacle. The space is organized around a central area where the defendant (the Knave) is held in chains. A prominent table holds evidence (tarts). There are designated areas for the jury (a mix of animals and birds) and spectators (guinea-pigs).

**Lighting and Atmosphere Cues:** 
Bright, harsh daylight or intense overhead lighting that emphasizes the absurdity and tension of the trial. The atmosphere is chaotic, nonsensical, and high-strung, oscillating between formal judicial procedure and sudden outbursts of violence/shrieking.

**Scale Cues and Recurring Environmental Anchors:** 
- **The Central Table:** Holds the stolen tarts.
- **The Jury Slates:** Small handheld writing surfaces used by animal jurors.
- **The Witness Stand/Entryway:** Where characters like the Hatter and the Cook enter with eccentric props (teacups, pepper-boxes).
- **Scale Shift:** The environment must accommodate Alice's sudden physical growth, making the court feel increasingly cramped or small relative to her.

**Descriptive Noun Phrases:** 
Open-air judicial arena, chaotic animal jury, slate-writing creatures, oversized tea service, pepper-scented air, nonsensical courtroom, sprawling outdoor tribunal, frantic legal proceedings.
