**Location: The Queen’s Croquet-Ground**
A garden setting featuring a large rose-tree with white roses being painted red by three card gardeners (Two, Five, and Seven). The terrain is characterized by ridges and furrows.

**Events & Character Entrances/Exits:**
*   **Gardener Interaction:** Two, Five, and Seven are painting white roses red to avoid the Queen's wrath. They notice Alice watching.
*   **The Procession Entrance:** A grand procession enters:
    *   10 Soldiers (Clubs): Oblong/flat shapes with hands/feet at corners.
    *   10 Courtiers (Diamonds): Walking in pairs.
    *   10 Royal Children (Hearts): Jumping hand-in-hand.
    *   Guests: Including the White Rabbit (nervous, smiling).
    *   The Knave of Hearts: Carrying the King's crown on a crimson cushion.
    *   The King and Queen of Hearts.
*   **The Confrontation:** The Queen questions Alice’s presence. Alice defies the Queen's authority ("Nonsense!"). The Queen orders the gardeners to be turned over; they are revealed as cards.
*   **Execution Attempt/Subversion:** The Queen orders the gardeners' heads off. Three soldiers stay behind to execute them. Alice intervenes by hiding the three gardeners in a large flower-pot. The soldiers lie to the Queen, claiming the heads are gone.
*   **Croquet Game Commencement:** The Queen invites Alice to play. 
    *   **Equipment/Mechanics:** Balls are live hedgehogs; mallets are live flamingoes; arches are made by soldiers doubling themselves up on hands and feet.
    *   **Gameplay Chaos:** Players do not take turns; they quarrel and fight over hedgehogs. The terrain (ridges/furrows) and moving arches make play difficult. Alice struggles with a disobedient flamingo and escaping hedgehogs.
*   **The White Rabbit Subplot:** During the game, the White Rabbit whispers to Alice that the Duchess is under sentence of execution for boxing the Queen's ears.
*   **Cheshire Cat Appearance:** A grin appears in the air, followed by eyes, then ears, and finally a full head. Alice uses the Cat as a confidant.
*   **The King vs. The Cat:** The King approaches Alice and expresses dislike for the Cat. Alice defends it ("A cat may look at a king"). The Queen orders the Cat's execution. The King leaves to fetch the executioner.
*   **The Hedgehog Incident:** Alice leaves the Cat to find her hedgehog, which is fighting another hedgehog. Her flamingo flies into a tree; she retrieves it.
*   **The Executioner’s Dilemma:** A crowd gathers around the disappearing Cat head. A dispute arises between the King (anything with a head can be beheaded), the Executioner (cannot cut a head from no body), and the Queen (threatens mass execution if not settled).
*   **Resolution/Exit:** Alice suggests asking the Duchess. The Queen orders the Duchess to be fetched. The Executioner exits "like an arrow." By the time he returns with the Duchess, the Cat has completely vanished.
